Humans have evolved to work with each other. We have a complex social structure where we make groups and solve problems. We understand quorum and the importance of visibility and sharing information. We also improvise and don't need help at every step.

Software is broken

  • Softwares don't talk to each other. Humans act as glue to make software work with each other. We download PDFs from Gmail to read numbers in it, input these numbers in Excel, then apply formulas, and send the Excel over Gmail. Over a billion people in the world act as this glue today in their daily jobs.

  • Software cannot perform new tasks unless explicitly programmed to do so. You cannot ask Gmail to download all emails that have PDFs, because Google engineers didn't program Gmail to do that.

  • Software needs human supervision for nearly every step. It cannot independently adapt or problem-solve.

  • Software is hard and expensive to modify. Even simple changes require engineering work.

  • Today's AI is broken software. OpenAI has given us intelligence in a box in the form of ChatGPT, but it still needs human oversight and action. ChatGPT knows how to use Gmail, but can only output text instructions to do so. A human is still required to follow those instructions.

Humans are the glue

As a result, companies hire a lot of people to "glue" systems together. E.g. in a finance team at one of the companies in India, over 60 people are employed. 80% of their time is spent on doing repetitive tasks with software. If the software were not so broken, this could be done by 10 people.

Our vision

Tomorrow's software will do things on its own. Teams will be able to teach it how they do things, and it should do them. It will learn your workflows, processes and systems. It will work alongside humans to dramatically increase our productivity. New ways for humans to teach, interact, explain to software will emerge. Such software will empower employees to focus their talents on creative, strategic thinking, which would make it much easier for companies to scale their business.
